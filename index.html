Aegis Operation Manual & Legal Framework

1. Consent Guard (Prevention)

Aegis proactively intercepts text prompts sent to generative AI models. If a user attempts to generate non-consensual media, impersonate a public figure, or create illicit material, Consent Guard's NLU engine immediately flags the intent and hard-blocks the request.

2. Evidence Assist (Response)

If a victim discovers a suspicious image online, they can upload it to Evidence Assist. The tool uses advanced multimodal vision to detect pixel-level AI blending errors. It calculates a quantitative "Synthetic Probability Score" to verify manipulation.

Global & Indian Legal Framework (IT Act 66E & UN SDGs)

The creation and distribution of non-consensual synthetic media (deepfakes) is a severe criminal offense in India under Section 66E of the Information Technology Act, 2000 and relevant sections of the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS). Violators face up to 3 years imprisonment and hefty fines. Furthermore, combating digital violence aligns directly with the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs 5 & 16). Aegis bridges the gap between victims and these legal systems by automatically drafting compliant FIRs, ready to be submitted to cybercrime.gov.in.
